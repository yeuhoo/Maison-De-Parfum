# Company Website Blueprint: Perfume Ecommerce & CRM

This document compiles the finalized brand blueprint provided by the client, including Executive Summary, Brand Fundamentals, Business Model, Target Audience, and Product Catalog Strategy. It serves as the foundation for the 1-month project plan.

## 1) Executive Summary

**Business Objective:**  
Launch a premium yet accessible perfume brand that generates direct-to-consumer sales while positioning the Wedding Keepsake Perfume Bar as a luxury experiential offering across Australia.

**Project Objective:**  
Build a high-converting ecommerce website that drives perfume bar enquiries and maximises direct bottle sales with repeat purchase capability and bundle optimisation.

**Launch Target Date:**  
Target launch within 90 days from final brand approval.

**Phase Strategy:**  
**Phase 1 – MVP:**  
• Core ecommerce (30ml & 50ml Extrait de Parfum)  
• Wedding Perfume Bar enquiry funnel  
• Bundle pricing structure  
• Compliance pages  
• Basic email automation

**Phase 2 – Growth Features:**  
• Membership subscription ($45/month)  
• Seasonal limited releases  
• Early access launches  
• Refillable system (subject to feasibility)  
• Loyalty rewards

**Top 3 Success Outcomes:**

1. Consistent monthly Wedding Perfume Bar bookings.
2. Average Order Value above $110 through bundle optimisation.
3. Repeat purchase growth via scent rotation and membership.

## 2) Brand Fundamentals

**Brand Mission:**  
To create meaningful fragrance experiences through elevated extrait formulations and unforgettable wedding scent experiences.

**Brand Vision:**  
To become Australia's leading accessible luxury fragrance house known for performance, elegance, and experiential storytelling.

**Brand Positioning:**  
Luxury made accessible. High-concentration extrait formulations at attainable pricing.  
Modern identity over heritage legacy.

**Brand Personality:**  
• Sophisticated  
• Empowering  
• Confident  
• Modern  
• Refined

**Brand Promise:**  
Luxury performance without traditional luxury markups. Long-lasting, personally tested, and compliance-approved fragrances.

**Differentiators:**  
• All Extrait de Parfum concentration  
• Wedding Keepsake Perfume Bar experience  
• Membership with unreleased scents  
• Seasonal drops  
• Bundle-driven pricing psychology  
• Strict IFRA and AICIS compliance

**Non-Negotiables:**  
Tone – Elevated, confident, never discount-driven.  
Visual Identity – Minimal, refined, premium aesthetic.  
Storytelling – Focus on performance, identity, and sensory experience; avoid exaggerated claims.

## 3) Business Model Definition

**Primary Sales Model:**  
• Direct-to-consumer ecommerce  
• Wedding service bookings  
• Discovery samples  
• Subscription (Phase 2)  
• Limited seasonal drops  
• Bundles and gifting

**Revenue Streams:**  
• 30ml bottles  
• 50ml bottles  
• Future 100ml bottles  
• 10ml samples  
• Wedding packages  
• Membership subscription

**Average Order Value Target:**  
$110–$145

**Expected Gross Margin:**  
70–85% estimated depending on bottle size and bundle mix.

**Geographic Focus:**  
Phase 1 – Australia  
Phase 2 – New Zealand  
Phase 3 – Selected international markets

**Currency:**  
AUD primary; international pricing adjusted for FX and shipping buffer.

**Sales Channels:**  
Primary – Website  
Future – Marketplace and bridal vendor collaborations

**Return & Refund Assumptions:**  
• No returns on opened fragrances  
• Replacement for faulty items only  
• Wedding deposits non-refundable  
• Samples encouraged to reduce blind-buy risk

## 4) Target Audience & Customer Segments

**Primary Segment – Modern Bridal Luxury Seekers:**  
Age 24–35, primarily female decision-makers in Australia.  
Motivation: Unique wedding guest experience.  
Barriers: Price and execution quality.  
Channels: Instagram, Pinterest, wedding expos.  
Trust Drivers: Styling visuals, testimonials, professionalism.

**Secondary Segment – Young Luxury Explorers:**  
Age 20–30, mixed gender, urban.  
Motivation: Self-expression and identity.  
Barriers: Blind-buy hesitation.  
Channels: TikTok, Instagram.  
Trust Drivers: Reviews, extrait strength, bundle value.

**Secondary Segment – Emerging Fragrance Collectors:**  
Age 28–45, moderate-high income.  
Motivation: Build scent wardrobe.  
Trust Drivers: Performance, exclusivity, limited drops.

**Excluded Audience:**  
• Discount-only buyers  
• Mass-market fragrance shoppers  
• Brand-label focused luxury consumers

## Product Catalog Documentation (Perfume E-commerce)

### 1) Catalog Strategy & Objectives

**Purpose:**  
To sell effectively, educate clearly, reduce returns, and increase blind-buy confidence.

**Catalog Goals:**  
• Improve product discovery  
• Increase conversion rate  
• Increase AOV via bundles  
• Reduce mismatch-related returns

**Scope – Included:**  
• 30ml Extrait  
• 50ml Extrait  
• 10ml samples  
• Bundle sets

**Deferred:**  
• 100ml bottles  
• Refill system  
• Home fragrance

### 2) Product Taxonomy (Information Architecture)

**Department:**  
• Fragrances  
• Gifts  
• Discovery

**Category:**  
• Women  
• Men  
• Unisex

**Subcategories:**  
• Extrait 30ml  
• Extrait 50ml

• Gift Set

**Collection Logic:**  
By Scent Family – Floral, Woody, Amber, Fresh, Citrus, Gourmand

By Season  
By Price Band

• One primary category per SKU  
• Multi-collection assignment allowed  
• Editorial collection naming convention
